# Meeting prep packs for PMs: the fastest way to compound product insight

The best product managers walk into every meeting prepared. They know the customer's history, their open issues, their expansion potential, and the landmines to avoid. This preparation compounds: each meeting builds on the last, creating deeper relationships and better product decisions.

But preparation takes time. With 4-5 customer meetings per week, spending 30 minutes preparing for each adds up to 2-3 hours of context-gathering that could be automated.

Meeting prep packs solve this. They synthesize everything you need to know about an account into a single document, delivered before you walk into the room.

## What Makes a Great Meeting Prep Pack

A meeting prep pack should answer five questions:

### 1. Who am I meeting with?

- **Contact details**: Name, title, tenure at company
- **Meeting history**: Previous conversations, key topics discussed
- **Communication style**: Preferences noted from past interactions
- **Stakeholder map**: Who else is involved in decisions?

### 2. What's the account context?

- **Company overview**: Size, industry, use case
- **Contract details**: Plan tier, ARR, renewal date
- **Health indicators**: Usage trends, NPS scores, engagement
- **Expansion potential**: Seats, features, upsell opportunities

### 3. What issues are open?

- **Support tickets**: Active issues, resolution status
- **Feature requests**: What they've asked for, current status
- **Escalations**: Any P1/P2 issues in flight
- **Blockers**: What's preventing them from getting value?

### 4. What happened recently?

- **Recent calls**: Key points from last 2-3 conversations
- **Product updates**: Features shipped that affect them
- **Their news**: Company announcements, leadership changes
- **Industry context**: Market trends affecting their business

### 5. What should I discuss?

- **Talking points**: Topics to cover based on context
- **Questions to ask**: Gaps in our understanding
- **Risks to address**: Issues that might come up
- **Opportunities**: Upsell, case study, referral potential

## Anatomy of a Meeting Prep Pack

Here's what a complete prep pack looks like:

```markdown
# Meeting Prep: Globex Corp
## QBR with VP of Operations
**Date**: January 15, 2026 | 2:00 PM EST
**Attendees**: Sarah Chen (VP Ops), Mike Torres (IT Director)

---

## Account Snapshot

| Metric | Value | Trend |
|--------|-------|-------|
| ARR | $120,000 | → Stable |
| Health Score | 72/100 | ↓ from 81 |
| NPS | 7 | ↓ from 8 |
| Active Users | 145/200 | ↑ 12% |
| Renewal | March 15, 2026 | 60 days |

**Risk Level**: Medium 🟡
**Expansion Potential**: High (200 → 500 seats possible)

---

## Recent History

### Last Meeting (Dec 1, 2025)
- Discussed search performance issues
- Sarah expressed frustration with date filtering
- Committed to roadmap update by end of Q4
- **Follow-up needed**: We promised date filters update

### Support Activity (Last 30 Days)
- **3 tickets opened** (vs. 1 avg for similar accounts)
- Ticket #4521: Search date filters (Open, P2)
- Ticket #4498: Export timeout (Resolved)
- Ticket #4467: SSO configuration (Resolved)

### Product Usage
- Search queries up 34% (good engagement)
- Export usage down 12% (timeout issues?)
- New feature adoption: 23% using AI summaries

---

## Key Insights from Gong

### Pain Points Mentioned (Last 3 Calls)
1. **Search limitations** (mentioned 8 times)
   > "I spend more time searching than actually working"
   > - Sarah Chen, Dec 1 call

2. **Reporting gaps** (mentioned 4 times)
   > "We need better visibility into team usage"
   > - Mike Torres, Nov 15 call

3. **Mobile experience** (mentioned 2 times)
   > "Field team can't use this effectively on tablets"
   > - Sarah Chen, Oct 20 call

### Competitive Mentions
- Mentioned evaluating Competitor X in November call
- Specifically cited their date filtering as superior

### Expansion Signals
- Sarah mentioned "rolling out to West Coast team" if issues resolved
- Potential 300 additional seats ($180K ARR)

---

## Open Items

### Our Commitments
- [ ] Date filters roadmap update (overdue from Dec)
- [ ] Usage reporting dashboard (promised for Q1)
- [ ] Mobile improvements timeline

### Their Commitments
- [ ] Provide list of West Coast team requirements
- [ ] Schedule IT security review for expansion

---

## Talking Points

### Must Cover
1. **Date filters update**: Ship date confirmed for Feb 15
   - Acknowledge the delay, show progress
   - Demo the beta if possible

2. **Renewal conversation**: 60 days out
   - Gauge satisfaction level
   - Understand blockers to renewal

3. **Expansion discussion**: West Coast rollout
   - What do they need to move forward?
   - Timeline for their decision

### Questions to Ask
- How is the team adapting to the new AI features?
- What would make the mobile experience work for field team?
- Who else should we include in expansion planning?

### Risks to Address
- They may raise Competitor X comparison again
- Export timeout issue might still be frustrating
- Health score drop needs acknowledgment

### Opportunities
- Case study potential (if search issues resolved)
- Referral to similar companies in their industry
- Beta access to new features as goodwill gesture

---

## Preparation Checklist

- [ ] Review date filters beta demo
- [ ] Prepare usage report for their account
- [ ] Check with engineering on mobile timeline
- [ ] Have expansion pricing ready
```

## Building Prep Packs with AI Agents

Meeting prep is ideal for AI automation because it's:

- **Data-intensive**: Requires pulling from 5+ systems
- **Time-sensitive**: Needed before every meeting
- **Structured**: Follows a consistent format
- **Low-risk**: Review before use, no external writes

### Data Sources for Prep Packs

| Source | What It Provides |
|--------|------------------|
| CRM (Salesforce, HubSpot) | Account details, contacts, opportunities |
| Gong/Chorus | Call recordings, pain points, quotes |
| Zendesk/Intercom | Support tickets, resolution history |
| Product analytics | Usage data, feature adoption |
| Jira | Feature requests, roadmap status |
| LinkedIn/News | Company updates, personnel changes |

### Automation Workflow

1. **Trigger**: Calendar event detected 30 min before meeting
2. **Identify**: Match meeting attendees to account
3. **Gather**: Pull data from all connected sources
4. **Synthesize**: Generate prep pack with insights
5. **Deliver**: Send via Slack, email, or calendar attachment

### What AI Does Well

- Aggregating data from multiple systems
- Identifying patterns in call transcripts
- Surfacing relevant quotes and pain points
- Formatting consistent prep documents
- Highlighting risks and opportunities

### What Humans Add

- Strategic context (why this meeting matters)
- Relationship nuance (how to approach this person)
- Judgment calls (what to emphasize or avoid)
- Real-time adaptation (pivoting based on meeting flow)

## Common Prep Pack Mistakes

### Mistake 1: Information Overload

A 10-page prep pack defeats the purpose. Keep it scannable:
- One page for quick reference
- Expandable sections for deep dives
- Clear hierarchy of importance

### Mistake 2: Stale Data

Prep packs must be fresh. A pack generated yesterday might miss:
- This morning's support ticket
- Yesterday's product release
- Recent news about the company

### Mistake 3: Missing the "So What"

Data without insight is noise. Don't just list facts. Explain implications:
- **Bad**: "3 support tickets this month"
- **Good**: "3 support tickets this month (3x their average). Something's wrong"

### Mistake 4: No Action Items

Prep packs should drive action. Include:
- Specific talking points
- Questions to ask
- Commitments to make or follow up on

## FAQ

**Q: How far in advance should prep packs be generated?**
A: 30 minutes to 1 hour before the meeting. This balances freshness with preparation time.

**Q: What if I have back-to-back meetings?**
A: Generate packs for all meetings at the start of the day. Quick review between meetings.

**Q: How do I handle meetings with new prospects (no history)?**
A: Focus on company research, industry context, and similar customer patterns. Less history, more preparation questions.

**Q: Should prep packs include competitive research?**
A: Yes, especially if the account has mentioned competitors. Know what you're up against.

**Q: How do I get my team to actually use prep packs?**
A: Make them easy to access (Slack, calendar) and prove value quickly. One well-prepared meeting converts skeptics.

**Q: What about internal meetings?**
A: Prep packs work for internal meetings too: stakeholder updates, cross-team syncs, executive reviews. Adapt the template.
