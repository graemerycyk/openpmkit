# The enterprise PM toolkit: briefs, themes, PRDs, and sprint review packs

Enterprise product managers juggle an enormous amount of recurring work. Daily standups, weekly planning, sprint reviews, quarterly roadmaps; each requires preparation, synthesis, and documentation.

This guide covers the essential PM artifacts for enterprise teams and how AI agents can automate their creation while maintaining quality and traceability.

## The Core PM Artifacts

Let's start with what enterprise PMs actually produce:

### 1. Daily Briefs

**Purpose**: Start each day with a clear picture of what happened overnight and what needs attention.

**Contents**:
- Urgent items requiring immediate action
- Sprint progress and blockers
- Customer escalations and support trends
- Key Slack discussions and decisions
- Metrics snapshot

**Frequency**: Daily, delivered before standup

**Time investment (manual)**: 30-45 minutes

### 2. Weekly VoC Themes

**Purpose**: Synthesize customer feedback into actionable themes for the team.

**Contents**:
- Top themes ranked by frequency and sentiment
- Representative quotes with sources
- Trend analysis vs. previous weeks
- Connection to roadmap items
- Recommendations for action

**Frequency**: Weekly, typically Monday

**Time investment (manual)**: 3-4 hours

### 3. Meeting Prep Packs

**Purpose**: Arrive at customer and stakeholder meetings fully prepared.

**Contents**:
- Account history and health score
- Recent interactions and open issues
- Relevant product updates
- Talking points and risks
- Follow-up items from previous meetings

**Frequency**: Before each meeting

**Time investment (manual)**: 20-30 minutes per meeting

### 4. Sprint Review Packs

**Purpose**: Document what shipped, celebrate wins, and capture learnings.

**Contents**:
- Completed work with demos/screenshots
- Velocity and burndown analysis
- Customer impact and feedback
- Blockers encountered and resolutions
- Retrospective themes

**Frequency**: End of each sprint

**Time investment (manual)**: 2-3 hours

### 5. PRD Drafts

**Purpose**: Define new features with evidence, requirements, and success criteria.

**Contents**:
- Problem statement with customer evidence
- User stories and requirements
- Success metrics and acceptance criteria
- Technical considerations
- Open questions and assumptions

**Frequency**: As needed for new features

**Time investment (manual)**: 4-8 hours per PRD

### 6. Roadmap Alignment Memos

**Purpose**: Present options and trade-offs for strategic decisions.

**Contents**:
- Decision required and context
- Options with pros/cons
- Evidence supporting each option
- Recommendation with rationale
- Next steps if approved

**Frequency**: Quarterly or for major decisions

**Time investment (manual)**: 3-5 hours

## The Automation Opportunity

Add up the time investment across these artifacts:

| Artifact | Frequency | Manual Time | Monthly Total |
|----------|-----------|-------------|---------------|
| Daily Brief | 20x/month | 35 min | 12 hours |
| VoC Themes | 4x/month | 3.5 hours | 14 hours |
| Meeting Prep | 15x/month | 25 min | 6 hours |
| Sprint Review | 2x/month | 2.5 hours | 5 hours |
| PRD Drafts | 2x/month | 6 hours | 12 hours |
| Alignment Memos | 1x/month | 4 hours | 4 hours |
| **Total** | | | **53 hours** |

That's over a week of work per month just on recurring artifacts. And this doesn't include the context-switching cost of gathering information from multiple systems.

AI agents can automate 70-80% of this work:

- **Data gathering**: Fully automated
- **Synthesis**: Mostly automated with human review
- **Formatting**: Fully automated
- **Review and refinement**: Human judgment

The result: 10-15 hours per month instead of 53, with more consistent quality.

## Building Your PM Toolkit

Here's how to implement each artifact with AI assistance:

### Daily Brief

**Data sources**:
- Jira: Sprint tickets, blockers, velocity
- Slack: Key channel activity, decisions, @mentions
- Zendesk: New escalations, ticket trends
- Gong: Yesterday's customer calls

**Automation approach**:
1. Schedule job to run at 7 AM
2. Pull overnight activity from each source
3. Prioritize by urgency and relevance
4. Generate brief with action items
5. Deliver via Slack or email

**Human review**: Scan for accuracy, add personal context

### VoC Themes

**Data sources**:
- Zendesk: Support tickets
- Gong: Call transcripts and insights
- Community: Posts and feature requests
- NPS: Verbatim responses

**Automation approach**:
1. Aggregate feedback from past 7 days
2. Cluster semantically into themes
3. Score by frequency, sentiment, trend
4. Extract representative quotes
5. Generate report with recommendations

**Human review**: Validate theme labels, refine recommendations

### Meeting Prep

**Data sources**:
- CRM: Account history, health score
- Gong: Previous call recordings
- Zendesk: Open tickets for account
- Jira: Related feature requests

**Automation approach**:
1. Trigger 30 minutes before meeting
2. Pull account context from all sources
3. Identify risks and opportunities
4. Generate talking points
5. Deliver as prep pack

**Human review**: Add personal knowledge, adjust talking points

### Sprint Review

**Data sources**:
- Jira: Completed tickets, velocity
- GitHub/GitLab: PRs merged, contributors
- Confluence: Updated documentation
- Slack: Team discussions, blockers

**Automation approach**:
1. Trigger at sprint end
2. Aggregate completed work
3. Calculate velocity and trends
4. Summarize blockers and resolutions
5. Generate review document

**Human review**: Add narrative, highlight wins

### PRD Drafts

**Data sources**:
- Community: Feature requests, votes
- Gong: Pain points from calls
- Zendesk: Related support issues
- Analytics: Usage data, no-results queries
- Confluence: Existing documentation

**Automation approach**:
1. Trigger on demand with feature topic
2. Gather evidence from all sources
3. Structure into PRD template
4. Identify open questions
5. Generate draft with evidence sections

**Human review**: Refine requirements, add judgment calls

### Alignment Memos

**Data sources**:
- Jira: Roadmap items, dependencies
- Analytics: Feature usage, trends
- Community: Request votes, sentiment
- Competitor: Recent changes, gaps

**Automation approach**:
1. Trigger on demand with decision topic
2. Gather evidence for each option
3. Analyze trade-offs
4. Generate memo with recommendation
5. Include evidence links

**Human review**: Validate analysis, adjust recommendation

## Quality Principles

Automated artifacts must meet quality standards:

### 1. Traceability

Every insight should link to its source. "Search is the top pain point" should link to the 52 mentions across Zendesk, Gong, and community.

### 2. Recency

Data should be fresh. A daily brief with yesterday's data is useful; one with last week's data is not.

### 3. Relevance

Filter noise aggressively. Not every Slack message belongs in a daily brief. Focus on signal.

### 4. Actionability

Every artifact should answer "so what?" Include recommendations and next steps.

### 5. Consistency

Use consistent templates and formatting. Stakeholders should know what to expect.

## FAQ

**Q: Will automated artifacts feel generic?**
A: Good automation preserves your voice and context. Templates should reflect your team's style, and human review adds the personal touch.

**Q: What about sensitive information?**
A: Implement proper access controls. Not everyone should see customer escalation details or competitive research.

**Q: How do I measure success?**
A: Track time saved, artifact quality (via team feedback), and coverage (are you producing artifacts you couldn't before?).

**Q: What if the AI gets something wrong?**
A: Draft-only mode ensures you review before anything is shared. Errors stay in drafts, not in stakeholder inboxes.
